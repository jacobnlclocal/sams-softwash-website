Rust Removal

Specialized treatments that eliminate stubborn rust stains from any surface

Eliminate Stubborn Rust Stains

If you have well water, a sprinkler system, or use iron-rich fertilizer in Florida, you've probably seen those frustrating orange rust stains appear on your driveway, sidewalk, or even your home's siding. These stains are notoriously difficult to remove - regular pressure washing often just spreads them around.

Our rust removal service uses specialized, professional-grade rust removers that chemically neutralize iron oxide stains. Combined with our cleaning equipment, we can remove most rust stains completely without damaging your surfaces.

Common Causes of Rust Stains

  • Sprinkler overspray - High-iron well water creates orange streaks
  • Fertilizer runoff - Iron-rich lawn fertilizers stain concrete
  • Metal furniture/planters - Rust transfers to surfaces below
  • Battery acid - Leaves orange-brown stains on driveways
  • A/C condensate - Drip lines with iron pipes leave rust trails

Surfaces We Treat

Concrete

Driveways, sidewalks, patios, and garage floors. Our treatment penetrates porous concrete to lift stains from deep within.

Pavers

Brick and concrete pavers respond well to our rust removal process without affecting the paver color or sealer.

Siding

Vinyl, stucco, and painted siding can all be treated for rust stains, typically from sprinkler overspray.

Stone

Natural stone like travertine, flagstone, and limestone require careful treatment, which we're trained to provide.

Our Rust Removal Process

1

Assessment

We evaluate the stain severity, surface type, and likely cause to choose the right treatment approach.

2

Apply Rust Remover

We apply professional-grade rust remover that chemically converts iron oxide, changing stains from orange to clear.

3

Agitation

For stubborn stains, we agitate the treatment to work it deeper into the surface for better penetration.

4

Rinse & Neutralize

We thoroughly rinse and neutralize the surface, leaving it clean and safe.

What to Expect

Rust removal results vary based on:

  • Stain age - Fresh stains remove more easily than old ones
  • Surface porosity - Some surfaces absorb rust deeper than others
  • Stain severity - Heavy stains may need multiple treatments

We're always honest about expected results before we begin. Some very old or severe stains may be significantly lightened but not completely removed.
